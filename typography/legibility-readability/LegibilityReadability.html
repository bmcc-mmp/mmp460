Legibility & Readability

Legibility refers to the ease with which a reader can recognize and differentiate between letterforms.

Readability refers to how easily a page of text can be read and navigated.

Factors that affect legibility:

Style of a Typeface

  • Familiarity
  • Serif vs. Sans Serif
  • Typeface style
  • Color and Value
  • Typographic color
  • Texture of type

Size and Shape of Type

  • Stroke weight
  • Contrast
  • Set width (width of letters)
  • Point size

Background

  • Contrast between text and background
  • Texture of background
  • Surface (of paper or screen)

Factors that affect readability:

Arrangement of Letterforms

  • Number of typefaces
  • Alignment
  • Measure
  • Widows and orphans

Use of negative spaces in a layout

  • Letterspacing, tracking, and kerning
  • Word spacing
  • Leading

Your Audience

  • Physiology of reading: eye focusing
    and reading gravity
  • Spelling
